## 1. The competitive landscape — three tiers

### Tier A — Mega brands (unbeatable head-on)
| Site | What we know | What they own |
|---|---|---|
| calculator.net | ~58M visits/mo, ~51% Google organic (Semrush profile, unverified) | Generic "X calculator" head terms |
| Bankrate | Mortgage cluster ~4.4M organic sessions/mo; ~1,065 pages in /mortgages/; flagship calculator = 25.9% of subfolder traffic (case-study figures, unverified) | All mortgage head + rate terms |
| NerdWallet | Top calculator = 33.7% of /calculators/ traffic; ranks with programmatic [state] mortgage pages | Head terms, affordability |
| SmartAsset | ~1.1M keywords; per-state calculator template is the category standard | State-level finance terms |
| Zillow / Redfin / Chase / Rocket / US Bank / Ramsey | Brand + DR90 | Head terms, brand-adjacent queries |

We do not compete with Tier A on any primary keyword. They are the ceiling
that keeps head terms out of the plan.

### Tier B — Mid-tier specialists (our medium-term benchmark)
mortgagecalculator.org (exact-match domain, variant-page playbook),
financialmentor.com, credible.com, finder.com, SoFi/Experian content hubs,
undebt.it (indie debt tool that ranks page-1 nationally).
These prove a *focused tool site* — not a bank, not a media giant — holds
page-1 positions in this niche.

### Tier C — The revealing tier (why there's room for us)
Live July-2026 page-1 results for our target keywords include:
- **Tiny community banks & credit unions** running generic white-label
  calculator widgets: mct.bank, southwest.bank, bog1.com (Bank of
  Greeleyville), trucommunity.bank, cibmbank.com, pittsfordfcu.org,
  fairwinds.org, rcu.org
- **A state-government finance-literacy page** (idfpr.illinois.gov)
- **A raw Excel file** hosted on a nonprofit's WordPress uploads folder
- **Small local lenders' blog posts**: dsldmortgage.com, loanpronto.com,
  realpha.com

When page 1 has room for a .xls download and a five-branch bank's stock
widget, Google is not demanding DR90 — it's starving for a genuinely good
page. That is the gap this site is built for.

## 2. SERP evidence by keyword tier (sampled July 2026)

### Tier 1 — Variant calculators → **HIGH opportunity** ✅
- **"biweekly mortgage calculator"** — page 1: Bankrate,
  mortgagecalculator.org, financialmentor, then FIVE small bank/CU/gov
  pages with white-label widgets (mct.bank, southwest.bank, idfpr.illinois.gov,
  bog1.com, trucommunity.bank, vmf.com). Half of page 1 is weak.
- **"debt snowball calculator"** — page 1: Ramsey, financialmentor, then
  Pittsford FCU, an App-Store listing, Fairwinds CU, cibmbank.com, an Excel
  file, finred.usalearning.gov, undebt.it. Only ONE Tier-A brand present.
- **"mortgage calculator with extra payments"** — stronger (Bankrate,
  Pennymac, Ramsey, US Bank, Experian, calculator.net) but still seats
  credit unions (rcu.org) and vmf.com.

The white-label widgets that fill these SERPs have no amortization tables,
no shareable URLs, no comparison views, thin copy — exactly the quality
gaps CLAUDE.md §4.1 requires us to beat.

### Tier 2 — Programmatic amount pages ("$300,000 mortgage payment") → **MEDIUM** ⚠️
Page 1: Credible, Chase, SoFi (×2 pages!), Yahoo Finance, AmeriSave,
finder.com, realpha.com, Redfin, Zillow. Strong names — **but almost all
are static articles, not interactive calculators.** SoFi holding two slots
and realpha.com ranking shows the SERP isn't fully consolidated. Our
differentiator: pre-filled interactive calculator + rate×term payment
matrix + income-needed section per page. Win expectation: positions 4–10
long-tail pickup first ("300k mortgage payment 15 year", "at 7%"), not
instant top-3.

### Tier 3 — Question long-tails ("how much income for a 400k house") → **MEDIUM-LOW as click source** ⚠️
Page 1: rate.com, Redfin, Better, SoFi, CNBC, Fortune, SmartAsset — plus
small lenders (dsldmortgage.com, loanpronto.com). Small sites do crack this
SERP, but this query class carries the heaviest AI-Overview absorption
(−61% CTR on AIO queries, Seer 2025, unverified). Role of our guides:
AIO citation + cluster authority + funnel into tools — not primary traffic.

### Tier 4 — State pages → **MEDIUM** (unchanged from SEO_PLAN)
SmartAsset/NerdWallet own the pattern, but 50-state long-tails
("[state] mortgage calculator with taxes") have room for a data-rich page.
Slowest tier to mature; needs domain trust first — correctly sequenced
after M1–M2.5 in the build order.

## 3. Honest overall verdict

**Yes — the insertion opportunity is real, with conditions.**

Working for us: (a) page-1 slots in our exact target keywords are held by
white-label widgets and Excel files today; (b) our tool quality bar beats
those incumbents by design; (c) calculator intent is the most
AI-Overview-resistant query class; (d) a focused site (undebt.it,
mortgagecalculator.org, financialmentor) demonstrably can hold this niche.

Working against us: (a) zero domain history in the strictest YMYL
category — expect a Google "sandbox" period of roughly 3–6 months of weak
impressions regardless of quality; (b) no backlinks yet, and Tier-2/4
keywords won't move until some exist; (c) overall click supply is
shrinking (−41% CTR even without AIO, unverified).

Realistic trajectory (directional, not a promise): first impressions
months 1–3; first meaningful clicks from Tier-1 variant calculators months
4–8; programmatic tiers compounding months 6–12. The 12-month 50k/mo goal
requires most of the ~145-page plan shipped and indexed by around month 8,
plus a handful of natural backlinks (the snowball-vs-avalanche and
amount-page assets are the most linkable). If at month 6 Tier-1 pages have
impressions but no page-1 positions, the correct move is more/better
Tier-1 pages and outreach — not more programmatic pages.

## 4. What we must copy vs. avoid (per competitor)

| Competitor | Copy | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| mortgagecalculator.org | One URL per variant; deep supporting copy under the tool | Dated UI; .php URL clutter |
| SmartAsset state pages | Real per-state tax/insurance data wrapped around the tool | Their lead-gen popups (we monetize with ads, keep UX clean) |
| Ramsey | Opinionated, readable explanations; strong internal funnel tool→content | Preachy tone; hiding the schedule behind email capture |
| undebt.it | Focused single-purpose excellence | Requiring signup for core value |
| calculator.net | Breadth + speed | Zero explanation content — that's the gap we fill |
| White-label bank widgets | (nothing) | Being that: no tables, no share URLs, no context |
